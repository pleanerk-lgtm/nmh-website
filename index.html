Why precious metals

Your money is losing value. Metal is not.

South African investors face a currency that has lost over 60% of its value against the dollar in the last decade. Physical precious metals are priced in USD, held outside the rand and outside the financial system.

Inflation hedge
Gold has maintained purchasing power across centuries. While the rand loses value each year, gold priced in rand has delivered consistent long-term returns.
Portfolio diversification
Precious metals have low correlation to equities and bonds. Adding physical gold or platinum reduces portfolio volatility without sacrificing long-term returns.
Crisis protection
Physical metal has no counterparty risk. Unlike cash, equities or bonds, it cannot be defaulted on, frozen or devalued by a third party.
Rand currency risk
The rand has lost roughly 70% of its value against the dollar since 2010. Gold held in Sandton is priced in USD. When the rand weakens, your holding rises automatically.
SA has the supply
South Africa produces more platinum than anywhere on earth and is a top-five gold producer. Buying from NMH means no import premium.
Estate planning
Physical metal inside SA endowment wrappers allows direct beneficiary nomination, bypassing the estate and executor entirely.

The house · About NMH

We removed the middlemen.
All of them.

National Metals House was built by people who spent two decades inside the metals industry at the mine gate, in the smelter yard, on the trading desk and across the secondary materials chain throughout Sub-Saharan Africa and global markets. That experience taught one thing clearly: an extraordinary amount of value disappears in the middle. Not through inefficiency. Through structure. Through layers of brokers, intermediaries, agents and aggregators each taking a cut between the point where metal leaves the ground and the point where it reaches the person who needs it.

We built NMH to dismantle that structure. Not partially. Entirely. We sign offtake at the orebody. We route the refine directly. We hold the stock in a bonded warehouse in Sandton. And we deliver it out of two doors: tonnes to industrial buyers on long-term indexed contracts and grams to South African investors on a monthly debit order. The same metal. The same chain. The same warehouse. Two doors.

The retail side matters particularly. South African investors have historically had access to metals only through ETFs, mining equity or expensive offshore custody structures. The physical metal, a bar with a serial number in a vault with your name on it, has been the preserve of institutions. We built the Stash and the bullion shop to change that. R500 a month should be enough to own real metal. It is now.

The wholesale side reflects two decades of counterparty relationships, supply chain knowledge and trading discipline across base metals, precious metals, PGMs, ferrous streams and battery materials. We understand how offtake is structured, how pricing risk is managed and how logistics across SADC and global routes actually work.

Why we built it

Three problems. One answer.

The broker problem
Every handover in a metals transaction is a spread event. Mine to refinery. Refinery to trader. Trader to buyer. Each step adds cost that lands on the end buyer. Two decades on trading desks across Southern Africa showed exactly where that value disappears. We built NMH to own every step and pass the savings through.
The access problem
Physical precious metals custody in South Africa has always required either a large minimum investment, an expensive offshore structure or a private bank relationship that treated metals as an afterthought. The R500 per month investor had no real option. The Stash exists because that was wrong and fixable.
The transparency problem
Most metals businesses obscure their pricing. The spread is buried in the quote. The custody fee is buried in the annual statement. We print our margin on the invoice. We publish our audit report. We put the FSCA number on the page and we expect you to check it. Transparency is not a brand position. It is a structural commitment.
The SADC opportunity
Sub-Saharan Africa holds an extraordinary concentration of the metals the world needs most: gold, platinum, palladium, copper, aluminium, chromium, manganese and lithium. The gap between where metal comes out of the ground and where it enters global supply chains is where NMH operates. That is not a coincidence. It is twenty years of work.

How we operate

Four principles. Non-negotiable.

No pooling
Every retail gram is allocated to a named account. Your bar has a serial number and your name on the custody record. We never pool, lend or rehypothecate client metal.
No hidden spread
One markup, printed on the invoice. We buy at spot from the refinery and tell you exactly what margin we add. There is no secondary spread buried in the price.
No intermediaries
We own the offtake, the refine relationship, the warehouse and the delivery chain. Every broker we remove is margin that stays with you, whether you are buying a gram or a thousand tonnes.
Full audit trail
BDO audits the physical stockcount quarterly. The report is published to all custody clients. You can verify your holding is there before you ask us to verify it.
